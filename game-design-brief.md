## Gameplay

**Core loop:**
Reads the output and then makes selection

**Win/lose conditions:**
Open ended? I'm thinking like "The Oregon Trail".  Sometimes you make it but people die along the way, you rarely "win".

**Progression system:**
You play as Plato and you can input attributes or talents at the start of the game that can influence the "rolls" of trying to make progress with the tribe.

**Key mechanics (2-3 things that make it feel unique):**
1. You are not sure what happens next.
2. Systems based decisions don't always yield the expected outcome.
3. You can allocate authority to "sub-agents" or authority figures that have varying degrees of skills and helpfulness.
4. It can devolve quickly into chaos but we can also escalate to match the chaos.
5. Anarchistic government with a central source of power.

## MVP Definition

**What's the smallest version that's fun?**
The enemy or goal is to reach a "stable" civilization

**Must-haves:**
1. There is always an option to retreat to the cave and escape the pain.
2. There are more extreme actions but they introduce more potential for chaotic results in the characters reactions.
3. Think of the older games like Roller Coaster Tycoon/Pharoah/Black and White for inspiration.

**Stretch goals:**
1. AI SDK where an LLM makes decisions for the plot or the result (quickly) it's not fully hardcoded in multiple story trees
2. ASCII art
3. Looking into other successful games and cloning concepts, establishing more of a terror/horror/thriller vibe to the game or allow the user to select the "levels" of low/med/high for these attributes.
